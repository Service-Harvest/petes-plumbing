Signs your garbage disposal needs repair

A disposal that hums but doesn't spin, one that trips the reset button repeatedly, or one that's gone completely silent are the most common complaints we hear. Leaking from the bottom of the unit, unusual grinding or rattling noises, or water backing up into the sink while the disposal runs are also clear signs something needs attention.

A persistent bad smell that doesn't go away with regular cleaning can also point to trapped food waste the disposal isn't fully clearing anymore.

What causes disposal problems

Jams are by far the most common cause, usually from something the disposal isn't built to handle well. Fibrous foods like celery, corn husks, and onion skins are notorious for wrapping around the blades instead of grinding cleanly, and we see this constantly in busy family households where the disposal handles a steady stream of daily food scraps.

Beyond jams, worn-out motor bearings, a failed seal that causes leaking underneath, or a burnt-out motor from repeated overloading are all common reasons a disposal stops working for good rather than just needing to be cleared.

What to try before calling a plumber

First, make sure the disposal is actually off, then check for a reset button on the bottom of the unit and press it once. Never put your hand inside the disposal. If it's jammed, some manufacturers include a hex-key opening on the bottom you can use to manually rotate the blades and free up a stuck jam.

If the unit hums but won't spin after that, or if it's leaking, it's time to call us rather than keep pressing the reset button repeatedly, which can shorten the motor's life.

Our garbage disposal repair process

We start by checking whether the issue is a clearable jam, a tripped internal breaker, or a genuine mechanical failure like a worn motor or failed seal. Most jams and electrical trips are quick, inexpensive fixes.

If the motor or seals have actually failed, replacement is usually the more practical option rather than repairing an older unit that's likely to fail again soon. We'll explain what we find and give you a clear price for either repair or replacement before doing any work.

When to call a professional

Call us if the disposal won't reset, if it's leaking from underneath, or if you hear grinding or rattling that sounds mechanical rather than like food waste. Leaks near an electrical appliance are also worth having a professional look at right away rather than working around.

If you're also considering putting in a new sink at the same counter, it can make sense to handle both together. Frequently asked questions

Why does my garbage disposal just hum instead of grinding?

A hum usually means the motor is receiving power but the blades are jammed. Pressing the reset button after clearing the jam often solves it, but if humming continues, the motor itself may need attention.

What foods should I avoid putting down the disposal?

Fibrous foods like celery, corn husks, and onion skins are common culprits for jams, along with grease and bones, which can damage the blades or clog the drain line.

Is it better to repair or replace my garbage disposal?

If the motor or seals have genuinely failed, replacement is usually the more practical choice. If it's a clearable jam or electrical trip, repair makes more sense. We'll tell you honestly which applies.

My disposal is leaking underneath the sink — is that serious?

Yes, it's worth addressing quickly. A leak usually means a failed seal, and left unaddressed it can damage the cabinet underneath your sink.

How long do garbage disposals typically last?

It varies with use and household size, but heavy daily use in a busy kitchen tends to wear one out faster than light, occasional use.

Do you offer same-day garbage disposal repair?

Yes, whenever possible, since a non-working disposal affects how you can use your kitchen sink day to day.
