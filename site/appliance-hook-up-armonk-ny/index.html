How we hook up your appliance

We start by checking your existing supply line, shutoff valve, and drain connection to make sure they're in good shape and the right fit for your new appliance. Older shutoff valves in particular are a common weak point that's easy to miss until the new unit is already in place.

From there, we connect the water supply line, drain line, and any ice maker or water dispenser line, then test everything under full water pressure before we call the job done. If anything doesn't look right with the existing connections, we'll tell you before we finish, not after.

What affects the cost

A straightforward hook-up to an existing, well-maintained connection is a quick job. Cost goes up if we need to replace an old or corroded shutoff valve, add a new line where one doesn't exist, or work around a tight installation space.

We'll always look at the connection first and give you a clear price before doing any work beyond the basic hook-up.

New appliances in Armonk homes

We get a lot of calls around new appliance deliveries in Armonk and nearby towns, especially washing machines, dishwashers, and refrigerators with built-in ice makers. Delivery crews often aren't licensed to do plumbing connections, and a rushed DIY hook-up is one of the more common causes of a slow leak that goes unnoticed until it's damaged a floor or cabinet.

If you're relocating a washer during a basement finishing project rather than just replacing one in place, that's more involved than a standard hook-up — see setting up plumbing for a full laundry room for what that work involves.

Consumer Reports publishes helpful buying guidance for major appliances if you're still deciding which unit to get — we're happy to talk through the plumbing side once you've made your choice.

Avoiding leaks down the road

Most appliance leaks come down to a loose fitting, a worn washer, or an old shutoff valve that wasn't replaced when it should have been. We use proper fittings and test connections under real pressure, not just a quick visual check.

If your existing fixtures are older and due for attention, it's worth also asking about swapping out an old plumbing fixture while we're already on site.

When to call a pro

Call us before you connect a new washing machine, dishwasher, or fridge yourself, especially if your home has older plumbing or you're not sure the existing shutoff valve is in good condition. It's much easier to fix a bad connection before the appliance is installed than after.

If you're dealing with a separate issue like fixing a malfunctioning garbage disposal or thinking about adding a whole-home water filtration system, we can often handle it in the same visit.

Frequently asked questions

Do you hook up appliances that were delivered by someone else?

Yes. Most delivery crews don't handle the plumbing connection, so we're often called in separately to finish the hook-up correctly.

Can you connect a refrigerator's ice maker line?

Yes, we run and connect ice maker and water dispenser lines as part of a fridge hook-up.

What if my shutoff valve is old or stuck?

We'll let you know if it needs to be replaced before we connect the new appliance. Trying to hook up to a bad valve usually just causes problems later.

Do you offer same-day appliance hook-up?

We offer same-day appointments whenever we can, especially if your new appliance is already sitting in your kitchen or laundry room.

How long does a typical hook-up take?

A straightforward hook-up is usually quick. It takes longer if we need to address an old valve or add a new line.

Can you hook up multiple appliances in one visit?

Yes, we can connect several new appliances in the same visit if you're doing a kitchen or laundry room update all at once.
